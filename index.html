Background

About Me

I got into security because I was curious about how things break. That curiosity turned into a career — first as a Security Engineer at Confidential, where I conducted security reviews, built automated scanning tools, and led the security team, and now at Meta, where I help secure one of the world's largest platforms.

Outside of work, I spend my time hunting bugs and sharing what I learn. I believe the best way to get better at security is to stay hands-on — breaking things, writing about it, and helping others do the same. That's what drives the write-ups, the content, and the community work.

Whether it's a logic flaw hiding in a JavaScript file or a path traversal in a GraphQL endpoint, I find the most interesting bugs by going deeper than the surface level. If you're into security research or just getting started, I hope something here helps you on your journey.
